SENTINEL-FIN REGULATORY FRAMEWORK v2026.1

SECTION A: BALANCE ANOMALIES (RULE-101)
- Flag "Account Depletion": Any transaction that reduces the originating account balance by more than 90% in a single event.
- Flag "Initial Balance Mismatch": Transactions where the 'amount' and 'oldbalanceOrg' are identical, suggesting a total sweep of funds.
- Indicators: A 'newbalanceOrig' of zero is a primary indicator of unauthorized takeover or total fund liquidation.

SECTION B: TRANSACTION BEHAVIOR (RULE-202)
- High Risk: TRANSFER type transactions (encoded 4) that occur in a single burst exceeding $50,000.
- Suspicious: CASH_OUT events (encoded 1) following a large transfer or resulting in total account depletion.
- AML Pattern Recognition: High-ratio account depletion via TRANSFER or CASH_OUT must be flagged as potential layering or integration phases of money laundering.

SECTION C: REPORTING MANDATE
- All Rule-101 and Rule-202 violations require a "Mandatory SAR Filing" (Suspicious Activity Report).
- Auditors must justify flags by referencing the balance depletion ratio and the transaction type.
